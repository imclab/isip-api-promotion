No more manual updates

At the end of the CITES CoP15 there were 14,000 changes to species listings. All 175 [check number] parties management authorities needed to update their databases to match the changes. What if this happened automatically with no effort at all?

Same quality, same data

The species data curated by UNEP-WCMC undergoes a strict quality control process. By embedding these data into national databases directly, the quality and timeliness is maintained and is instantly comparable across the international secretariats and parties.

What needs to be done?

Building on the reengineered CITES databases, UNEP-WCMC aims to raise funds to go one step further. With $400,000 UNEP-WCMC with CITES support can build the necessary technology to allow members to embed the species list directly within their own databases.
