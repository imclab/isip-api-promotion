No more manual updates

Following CITES CoP15 there were over 14,000 changes to species listings, plus changes to species names and trade restrictions. With "computer to computer" communication, the need for all 177 Parties to update their national systems by hand can be avoided.

Same quality, same data

The species data compiled by UNEP-WCMC undergoes a strict quality control process. By embedding these data into national databases directly, the quality and timeliness is maintained and and the duplication of effort across the CITES community is avoided.

How do we make it happen??

Building on the reengineered CITES Species and Trade Databases, UNEP-WCMC hopes to develop an innovative tool for delivering CITES data. We are looking for support from the CITES community to make this happen. Please let us know if this is something that your country might be interested in learning more about.
